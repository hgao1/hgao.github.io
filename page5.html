data can be used to tell a story

In the summer of 2020, during the Black Lives Matter protests, a group of us decided that we wanted to find a way to help contribute to the movement given our specific background (physics). We noticed that a lot of statistics were being passed around, in pretty instagram info-slides, and we thought that we could contribute in some way on that front. 
So, we lauched our website, where we analyze previously written papers about police interactions with civilians. We attempt to explain to an audience (with little to no math background) the measures taken by the researchers in order to draw the conclusions that they draw. We also highlight "shortcomings", assumptions, etc... about these papers in order to give the audience a comprehensive view of what's being done, why it's being done, and what it all means.
