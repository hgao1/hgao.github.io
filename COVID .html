COVID-19 Data analysis

When New York City announced its lockdown beginning in mid-March, I began to work with Professor Bud Mishra of NYU Courant as part of a group that would build a more sophisticated SIR model and attempt to create a policy that also incorporated the economy. 

RxCOVEA

I joined Professor Mishra under a subgroup looking specifically at the best ways to model the spread of this infection. Interested in this group? Check this out. 

S-I-E.......-R Model

Often, an S-I-R model is the basic mathematics associated with plotting the spread of a disease, with a susceptible population (S), infected population (I), and recovered population (R). We created many variations of this model, with varying degrees of complexity: including an exposed popuation (SIER), an infected-asymptomatic and asymptomatic exposed population (S, IA, E, AE, R), and an unknown/unknwon exposed/unknown recovered population (S, IA, AE, U, UE, ..., UR, R). Creating these models and modeling how they evolved with time depended on developing a more comprehensive knowledge of non-linear dynamics (thanks, Strogatz!). Interested in this code? Find it on my github, or on Jantine's github! 

We have on the left, a S(susceptible)-I(infected)-IA(infected asymtopmatic)- E (exposed)-U(unknown)-UE(unknown exposed)- UA(unknown asymtopmatic)-UR(unknown recovered)-R(recovered) model. What a mouthful! 

...+ Economy?

we use Alvarez, Argente, and Lippi's paper "A Simple Planning Problem for COVID-19 Lockdown" as a guideline to our more complicated SIR model in order to try to create a function that balances human life with economic output. This formulation, itself, comes with its own moral questions (aka: how do you assign a monetary value to life?) that should be addressed. This is still a work-in-progress for the group.
